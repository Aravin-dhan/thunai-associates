Governance

Our policies and terms of engagement.

We are committed to transparency in how we handle information, conduct engagements, and use technology. These policies govern our relationship with all clients and partners.

Privacy

Privacy Policy

Information We Collect

Thunai Associates LLP collects personal and organisational information necessary to provide professional services. This may include names, contact details, identification documents, financial records, tax information, correspondence, and other information you provide in the course of an engagement or inquiry.

We may also collect information through our website, including contact form submissions, email correspondence, and basic usage data where required for communication purposes.

How We Use Information

Information collected is used solely for the purposes for which it was provided, including:

  • Providing legal, tax, and advisory services to clients
  • Responding to inquiries and maintaining client communication
  • Meeting statutory obligations including Know Your Client (KYC) requirements
  • Issuing invoices and managing billing
  • Complying with applicable law and regulatory requirements

We do not use client information for marketing purposes, and we do not sell or share personal data with third parties for commercial gain.

Data Retention

We retain client information for as long as required by law and professional standards. For tax-related records, this is typically a period of eight years from the relevant assessment year. Legal and advisory records are retained for a minimum of seven years from the date of engagement completion, unless a longer retention period is required by applicable statute.

Confidentiality

All information shared with Thunai Associates LLP in the course of an engagement is treated as strictly confidential. Our professionals are bound by obligations of professional confidentiality, and we do not disclose client information to third parties except as required by law, regulation, or with the express written consent of the client.

Third Parties

We engage third-party service providers only where necessary for the delivery of services (for example, registered valuation agents or government portals). Such providers are engaged under obligations of confidentiality and are permitted to use information only for the purposes of the specific task for which they are engaged.

Your Rights

You have the right to access personal information we hold about you, to request correction of inaccurate information, and to request deletion of information where no statutory or professional retention obligation applies. To exercise these rights, please contact us at thunaillp@gmail.com.

Governing Law

This policy is governed by the Information Technology Act, 2000 and applicable rules, and is aligned with the principles of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 as they come into force. Disputes are subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of courts in Chennai, Tamil Nadu.

Engagement

Terms of Engagement

Scope of Services

The scope of services provided by Thunai Associates LLP is defined by a written engagement letter or agreement entered into with each client at the commencement of an engagement. Services are limited to those described in the engagement documentation. Any extension or variation of scope requires written agreement.

Client Obligations

Clients are required to provide accurate, complete, and timely information necessary for the performance of services. Thunai Associates LLP accepts no liability for consequences arising from incomplete, inaccurate, or untimely information provided by the client. Clients must promptly inform us of any change in circumstances that may be relevant to an ongoing engagement.

Fee and Payment Terms

Fees are agreed upon at the commencement of each engagement and are set out in the engagement letter. Unless otherwise specified, invoices are payable within fourteen days of the date of invoice. Late payment may result in suspension of services. Out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the course of an engagement are billed at cost.

Limitation of Liability

Thunai Associates LLP's liability in respect of any engagement is limited to the fees paid for that specific engagement. We are not liable for indirect, consequential, or third-party losses arising from our services. Our advice is specific to the facts and law applicable at the time it is given and does not constitute ongoing advice in the event of subsequent changes in law, regulation, or the client's circumstances.

Intellectual Property

All work product, research, analysis, and documentation produced by Thunai Associates LLP remains our intellectual property unless expressly transferred by written agreement. Clients are granted a licence to use deliverables for the purposes for which they were commissioned. Work product may not be reproduced, distributed, or relied upon by third parties without our written consent.

Termination

Either party may terminate an engagement by providing written notice. Upon termination, the client is liable for fees for services rendered up to the date of termination plus any reasonable costs incurred. We reserve the right to terminate an engagement immediately in circumstances involving breach of these terms, professional ethics obligations, or applicable law.

Governing Law

These terms are governed by the laws of India. Any disputes arising out of or in connection with an engagement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of courts in Chennai, Tamil Nadu.

Compliance

Anti-Money Laundering & KYC Policy

Our Obligations

Thunai Associates LLP is subject to the Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 (PMLA) and applicable rules made thereunder. We are committed to full compliance with our obligations as a reporting entity and to maintaining high standards of due diligence in client onboarding and ongoing engagement management.

Know Your Client Requirements

Before commencing any engagement, we are required to verify the identity of clients and, where applicable, their beneficial owners. For individual clients, this requires a valid government-issued photo identity document (Aadhaar, PAN, passport, or driving licence) and proof of address. For entities, we require constitutional documents (certificate of incorporation, partnership deed, or trust deed), PAN, and identity documentation for authorised signatories and beneficial owners.

Ongoing Monitoring

We conduct periodic reviews of client information throughout the duration of an engagement. Clients are required to inform us promptly of any material change in their identity, beneficial ownership, or business nature. We reserve the right to seek updated KYC documentation at any time during an engagement.

Reporting Obligations

Where we have reasonable grounds to suspect that a transaction or activity involves the proceeds of crime or is connected to money laundering or terrorist financing, we are required by law to file a report with the Financial Intelligence Unit – India (FIU-IND). The making of such a report is subject to tipping-off restrictions and cannot be disclosed to the subject of the report.

Ethics

Conflict of Interest Policy

Conflict Checks

Prior to accepting any new engagement, Thunai Associates LLP conducts a conflict of interest check against our current and former client records. This check is designed to identify any circumstances in which acting for a new client could compromise our ability to act in the best interests of an existing or former client, or in which we hold information that could disadvantage a new client.

Disclosure and Consent

Where a potential conflict is identified, we will disclose the nature of the conflict to all relevant parties and seek informed written consent before proceeding, where this is permissible under applicable professional rules. In cases where a conflict cannot be managed through disclosure and consent, we will decline to act for one or more of the affected parties and will assist in identifying alternative professional advisors.

Ongoing Obligations

Our professionals are required to report potential conflicts as they arise during the course of any engagement. Any professional who becomes aware of a conflict of interest or potential conflict is required to raise it promptly so that appropriate steps can be taken. We do not permit personal interests or relationships to influence the advice or services provided to clients.

Technology

AI Use & Ethics Policy

Our Position on AI

Thunai Associates LLP recognises that artificial intelligence tools offer potential benefits in the context of legal and advisory research. At the same time, we are acutely aware of the limitations, risks, and ethical considerations associated with AI-generated content in professional practice. This policy sets out how we approach the use of AI tools in our work.

AI as a Research Aid Only

We may use AI-assisted tools for preliminary research, summarisation, and background analysis. AI tools are used solely as a research aid and are never relied upon as the source of final legal, tax, or advisory opinion. All outputs from AI tools are reviewed, verified, and validated by qualified professionals before being incorporated into any client-facing work product or advice.

Human Review Requirement

Every piece of advice, opinion, analysis, or document delivered to a client is the product of professional human judgment. No AI-generated output is delivered to a client without review and sign-off by a qualified member of our team. The professional issuing the advice takes full responsibility for its accuracy and appropriateness.

Client Data and AI Tools

We do not upload, input, or share confidential client information, personal data, or sensitive documents into any third-party AI platform or service. Client confidentiality obligations are absolute and take precedence over any internal workflow consideration. Where AI tools are used for research purposes, only publicly available and non-confidential information is processed through such tools.

Transparency

Where AI-assisted research has contributed materially to work delivered to a client, we will inform the client of this fact upon request. We believe transparency on this point is consistent with the trust that underpins our professional relationships. Clients who prefer that no AI tools be used in their matter may communicate this preference to us, and we will honour it.

Evolving Standards

We monitor developments in the guidance issued by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI), the Bar Council of India, and other relevant professional bodies on the use of AI in legal and advisory practice. Our AI use policy will be updated as professional standards and regulatory guidance evolve. We are committed to adopting AI responsibly, in a manner consistent with our duties to clients and the integrity of the profession.
